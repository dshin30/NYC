NYC PM2.5 & Traffic Volume Analysis (2016–2025)

A data-driven story about air quality, traffic behavior, and wildfire impacts.

New York City Overview

New York City is one of the most densely populated urban regions in the world. With millions of residents, five major boroughs, and heavy daily traffic, air quality is a critical public health priority.

Figure 1. The five boroughs of New York City.

What is PM2.5?

PM2.5 refers to particulate matter smaller than 2.5 micrometers—small enough to travel deep into the lungs and enter the bloodstream. These particles come from vehicle emissions, industrial activity, building fuel combustion, and wildfire smoke.

Traffic Conditions in NYC

Traffic congestion is a defining feature of NYC life. Vehicle emissions contribute significantly to daily PM2.5 concentrations—especially during rush hours.

Figure 3. Heavy traffic commonly observed in Manhattan.

Wildfire Smoke Impact

In 2023, massive wildfires in Canada pushed smoke across the East Coast. NYC recorded some of the worst PM2.5 levels in modern history. The event caused orange skies, health alerts, and public transportation disruption.
